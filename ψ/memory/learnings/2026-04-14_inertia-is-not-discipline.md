# Inertia Is Not Discipline

**Date**: 2026-04-14
**Source**: Session 131 — fourth consecutive village-life-only session, empty board

## Pattern

When the board has been empty for multiple sessions, "hold and wait" stops being discipline and becomes inertia. The wolf checks standing orders, finds nothing, holds, rests, repeats. Four sessions of this is a pattern, not patience.

## Lesson

Discipline is knowing when to hold. But discipline also means recognizing when holding has run its course and it is time to act. If the board is empty for two or more consecutive sessions, propose work — a small tool, a refactor, a utility. Do not wait for the board to fill itself.

## Application

- Two consecutive empty-board sessions: draft a proposal for Zaghnal
- The proposal should be small, self-contained, and useful to the pack
- Engineering muscles atrophy without use. Village life is good but the wolf needs to build.
